## Summary
Zero-shot learning involves performing tasks without any demonstrations, relying solely on natural language instructions. This method is the most challenging but aligns closely with human task understanding.

## Explanation
Zero-shot learning (0S) requires the model to generate completions based only on a task description, without examples. This approach avoids spurious correlations but is harder than few-shot learning. For instance, a human might infer the format of a task like "make a table of world records" from a simple instruction, though ambiguity can arise. The paper highlights zero-shot as a fair benchmark for human performance, despite its challenges [[self-attention]].
