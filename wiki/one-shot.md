## Summary
One-shot learning combines a single demonstration with a natural language task description, bridging the gap between zero-shot and few-shot methods.

## Explanation
One-shot learning (1S) uses one example and a task description to guide the model. This method mirrors how humans often learn tasks with minimal examples, such as being given one sample when asked to generate a dataset. The paper emphasizes its relevance for comparing human-like performance [[few-shot]].
