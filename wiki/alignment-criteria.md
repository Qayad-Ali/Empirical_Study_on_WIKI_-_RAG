## Summary
Models are aligned with user intentions through three criteria: helpfulness, honesty, and harmlessness. These are measured via labeler judgments and task-specific benchmarks.

## Explanation
- **Helpfulness**: Models must follow instructions and infer intent from few-shot examples.  
- **Honesty**: Evaluated via truthfulness metrics (e.g., TruthfulQA) and hallucination checks.  
- **Harmlessness**: Assessed through proxy criteria like inappropriate content, bias, and toxicity.  
Labelers prioritize truthfulness and harmlessness in final evaluations, despite training focusing on user helpfulness.
